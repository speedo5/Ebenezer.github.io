Ebenexer Home

Ebenezer Happy Home sits on Mama Mary Aoko Otieno’s farm at Ramba Village, on Mfangano Island in the middle of Lake Victoria. It is in South Nyanza province in the Western region of Kenya approximately 500km West of Nairobi-Kenyan’s capital city.

Mama Mary did not have any good house except for a grass-thached mad house that her husband left her in as a widow in 1999. The house was leaking and Mary could sometimes pray that it does not rain in the night for fear of the house being flooded both from the leaking roof and the surface runoff from the nearby stream.

Her main prayer was “God give me a house big enough to accommodate my children and grandchildren” By God’s Grace a team of good Samaritans from Catholic Church visited the Island in 2002 and were going round homes to identify people to support. They quickly build Mary a small two room’s house to relocate her from the worn-out, leaking, inclined and almost falling grass thatched mud house. She lived in the house for close to 20 years. In 2020, her children build her the current spacious 3 bedroom house which she gladly shares with the orphaned children from the community as their own home.

On the same compound sits a free Methodist church building attended be a small local congregation from the local community. Mary extended her welcome to the children to live with her in her home after noticing a great poverty that the children were going through. These children who attended Sunday school in the church could over stay back in the church building long after the end of Sunday

Ebenexer Home is currently reaching out to twenty children around The whole Island. We are trusting God that we will reach a larger number through your support. The children are in different levels of education.
